Ian Fleming

Creator of James Bond

Ian Fleming is best known as the creator of James Bond, a British Secret Service agent codenamed 007. Fleming was an English journalist, author, and naval intelligence officer during the Second World War. James Bond is based on a combination of people he met during his time with the Naval Intelligence Division. Fleming modeled some characteristics of Bond after his brother, Peter, and Conrad O'Brien-ffrench, a British Secret Intelligence Officer he met while skiing in the 1930's.

Fleming released his first spy novel, Casino Royale in 1952. He went on to pen eleven novels and several short-stories starring James Bond. James Bond's escapades translated successfully to film with the relase of Dr. No. in 1962. Fleming's legacy continues to thrive through the character he created. James Bond has been featured in over twenty films, and has been played by seven actors.
